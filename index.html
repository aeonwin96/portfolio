About

My journey into technical writing started one sunny morning in Logan, Utah, circa 2014. It was graduation day at USU. I recall standing at the metaphorical precipice, clutching my degrees, while projecting a thousand-yard stare directly at the horizon. But despite my earnest attempt to get lost in the pomp and circumstance of the day's ceremony, I was fast aware of a developing inability to look beyond the rhetorical edge, where my promising finance career was calling up from the ravine below.

Later that fateful day, an old friend reached out to me about a job opportunity in SEO and Content Management. The skills I learned there helped me move onto a position managing pools of freelance writers, creating technical content for marketing purposes. Both of those position were the foundation for securing my role in API documentation at Adobe Workfront. During my tenure, I've grown more than at any other time in my career. Now, when I look to the horizon, I'm excited to see all of the opportunities that lie ahead.

What's new in API version 13

What's new in API version 13is an example of a reference article intended to inform a developer audience of all changes that occurred between each version of the Core API. I inherited this series starting with version 9 in early 2019. After working on that release, I realized that the original format wasn't exactly useful for our intended audience. During the subsequent releases, I began reworking the format and made significant improvements. However, the current iteration didn't take shape until I had been assigned some intern hours and used these documents as a way to help familiarize them with the Core API. These exchanges were fun learning opportunities and provided me with valuable insight that continues to inform how I approach documentation through today.
